Light in our Community

Our mission is to shine the light of Christ in our community, through programs that stabilize and enrich the lives of babies, young adults and seniors. We also support other community outreach programs by providing rent-free office space at our downtown location. Won't you join our efforts by donating your resources, time and talents?

About Us


How We Help Our Community

We bridge gaps in services for working families and retirees. Saint Saviour's Center is a community outreach organization that addresses largely unmet needs for low-income residents of Wake County. We serve families and seniors who have an income, yet struggle to make ends meet. Each year, the Center assists more than 10,000 people who lack resources to overcome hardships associated with poverty, aging, hunger and homelessness. We also provide housing for affiliated nonprofits in the area. You can read more about them below. Annually, we host 4 separate fundraising events to ensure that we are able to continue our work in the community. We host a 10K in the Spring, Christmas Gala and Dinner/Live Auction each Fall. These events are not only necessary to continue our work, they are a lot of fun for both our volunteers and attendees.

Wake County Diaper Bank

The provision of disposable diapers and wipes for the first year of a baby's life costs a staggering $1,104.00. Since 2010, Saint Savour's Center has worked diligently to offset these costs for struggling Wake County parents. Our effots are successful due to the generosity of many community groups, churches, schools and businesses in the area. The Diaper Train then distributes the diapers in two ways:

Volunteers deliver diapers to local, non-profit organizations that serve families in crisis or transition. Families with a referral from an approved referring agency may come to Saint Saviour's Center on Tuesday or Thursday between 10:00 a.m. and 12:00 noon to receive diapers. Referrals are required, and families must reside in Wake County, NC. Due to the program's success, it has a dedicated website. Please visit www.diapertrain.org for more information.


Super Seniors

Older adults need to stay physically, mentally and spiritually active in order to remain independent and maintain a high quality of life. Healthy Living for Seniors is a whole-person program for seniors in public housing who do not have access to resources they need. This program currently serves residents of Glenwood Towers and Carriage House communities in Raleigh. Our team of staff and volunteers offer classes on aging, with topics such as chronic illness, depression, loneliness, nutrition and exercise. Clergy and lay leaders from local churches assist with programs that address spiritual needs. Volunteer-led activities include arts and crafts, bingo, knitting and crochet, book club, walking groups, social events and outings. The Glenwood South Book Club meets on the first and third Mondays of each month at Glenwood Towers.



Housing for Non-Profits

Organizations that provide food, education and spiritual nurture expand the Center's ability to help people across Wake County from a single location. Saint Saviour's Center is home to: Meals on Wheels Congregate dining room where senior citizens receive a healthy lunch and fellowship, weekdays. Wake Relief Emergency groceries for those facing crises such as illness, loss of employment and financial hardship. Community Music School Music education for children whose parents cannot afford lessons. Haitian Evangelical Community Church Sunday services and Tuesday evening prayer meetings for Haitian Americans in their native language.
